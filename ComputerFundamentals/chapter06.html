Basics of Computer Networking

A computer network is a collection of interconnected devices that share resources and information. These devices can include computers, servers, printers, and other hardware.

1. What is a Computer Network?

A computer network is a group of interconnected devices that share resources and information efficiently.

2. Basic Terminologies of Computer Networks

  • Network: A group of connected computers and devices that can communicate and share data.
  • Node: Any device that sends/receives/forwards data (computers, servers, printers, etc).
  • Networking Devices: Devices that manage/support networking – routers, switches, hubs, APs.
  • Transmission Media: The physical/wireless medium (Ethernet cables, Wi-Fi, Fiber, etc).
  • Service Provider Networks: Networks offered by ISPs or carriers to lease access.

3. Types of Computer Network Architecture

  • Client-Server Architecture: Central server controls client nodes.
  • Peer-to-Peer Architecture: No central server, all nodes equal (P2P).

4. Network Devices

  • Router: Connects multiple networks, directs data packets.
  • Switch: Connects devices within a network, forwards data to intended device.
  • Hub: Connects multiple devices, sends data to all devices.
  • Bridge: Connects/filters traffic between networks, reduces network traffic.
  • Gateway: Connects different types of networks, translates protocols.
  • Access Point: Provides wireless connectivity, extends a wired network.
  • Modem: Converts digital data and provides internet access.
  • Firewall: Monitors and controls traffic for security.

5. Goals of Networks

  • Convenience for users.
  • Efficiency and performance.
  • Resource management (CPU, memory, storage).
  • Security & protection from unauthorized access.
  • Reliability & fault tolerance.
  • Scalability for growing needs.

6. Uses of Computer Networks

  • Communication (email, chat, video).
  • Resource sharing (printers, files, scanners).
  • Remote access to systems/data.
  • Collaboration tools/project sharing.
  • E-commerce and online payments.
  • Education: online learning, research.

7. Characteristics of Computer Networks

  • Security: Protection via firewalls, encryption, authentication.
  • Reliability: Redundancy, backups, high availability.
  • Scalability: Capacity to grow in devices/users.
  • High Performance: Fast transfer, low latency.
  • Quality of Service: Prioritization for smooth streaming/communication.
